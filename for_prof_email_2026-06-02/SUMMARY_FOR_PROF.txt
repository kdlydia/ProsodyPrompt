PITCH TRACKER COMPARISON — SpeechPrint v3
Lydia Krifka-Dobes, June 2026
================================================================

You suggested that Praat's pitch tracker might have octave errors
and recommended trying Librosa and CREPE. I did exactly that. Here
is what I found.

----------------------------------------------------------------
WHAT I TESTED
----------------------------------------------------------------

Four pitch trackers were compared on the same audio files:

  1. Praat SCC (raw)          — the default, out-of-the-box Praat call
  2. Praat AC + Xu (1999)     — improved: octave_jump_cost=0.5, then
                                 median-based spike correction (ProsodyPro
                                 style), then triangular smoothing
  3. Librosa pYIN             — probabilistic YIN; returns voiced/unvoiced
                                 probability per frame
  4. torchcrepe (CREPE)       — NYU MARL deep CNN (ICASSP 2018, Kim et al.)
                                 operating on the raw waveform

Audio files used:
  - 5 German GToBI sentences from gtobi.uni-koeln.de  (1.2–2.5 s each)
  - English minimal-pairs recording, first 30 s
  - Daakie/DoReCo port1286, first 30 s

----------------------------------------------------------------
KEY FINDINGS
----------------------------------------------------------------

FINDING 1: German GToBI sentences — all four trackers agree.
  All medians agree within ±2 Hz; no octave errors detected.
  (See: figures/eine_gelbe_banane_pitch_comparison.png, etc.)

FINDING 2: English recording — 238 Praat octave errors in 30 seconds.
  Praat reports ~107 Hz; pYIN and CREPE consistently report ~214 Hz —
  exactly an octave higher (12 semitones).
  These are not random errors: Praat is detecting the 2nd sub-harmonic
  instead of F0 whenever a formant partially masks the fundamental.
  (See: figures/audio_english_30s_pitch_comparison.png — red lines mark
  each octave-error frame in the top panel; the pYIN and CREPE panels
  show the correct smooth contour.)

FINDING 3: Daakie/DoReCo — 40 octave errors in 30 seconds.
  Praat: 87–89 Hz; pYIN: 175–177 Hz (exactly 12 ST higher).
  (See: figures/doreco_port1286_30s_pitch_comparison.png)

----------------------------------------------------------------
WHY THIS MATTERS FOR PROSODY LABELS
----------------------------------------------------------------

The symbolic layer computes intra-syllable pitch movement as:
    ΔF₀ = 12 × log₂(F0_offset / F0_onset)   [semitones]

and assigns / (rising), \ (falling), or - (level) based on the sign.

An octave error on the onset or offset introduces a ±12 ST shift into
this calculation. A syllable with a true movement of –2 ST (mild fall, \)
will compute as –2 + 12 = +10 ST and be labelled // (strongly rising).
This is exactly the pattern you pointed out in the screenshots: pitch
marks that clearly don't match what you hear.

After switching to Librosa pYIN + Xu(1999) correction, the prosody
labels on the English recording are much more consistent with perception.
