Basic	In one sentence: why is CREPE better than Praat for pitch tracking?	CREPE is a neural network trained on millions of pitch examples — it learned what pitch sounds like, not what it looks like mathematically. Praat uses autocorrelation and assumes the voice is harmonic. When phonation is irregular, CREPE is robust; Praat makes octave errors.	day3::trackers speechprint
Basic	What does pYIN give you that YIN does not?	A voiced/unvoiced probability for every frame. pYIN can say "I don't know." YIN always outputs a pitch value — even during silence. YIN's zero-unknown rate is a red flag, not a strength.	day3::trackers speechprint
Basic	Why is YIN's zero-unknown rate a red flag, not a good result?	YIN has no voiced/unvoiced detector. It returned 27,350 "voiced" frames out of ~28,000 total on the Daakie recording — 97.7%. It is producing pitch values during silence and consonants. That is not accuracy; it is recklessness.	day3::trackers speechprint
Basic	What are the two pipeline configurations and when is each used?	English/German ASR pipeline → pYIN (fast, no download, reliable V/UV detection). Daakie/DoReCo endangered-language pipeline → CREPE (no harmonic assumptions, most accurate, best for unknown phonation).	day3::trackers speechprint
Basic	What does "voiced/unvoiced detection" mean and why does it matter?	The tracker must decide whether a given frame contains speech (voiced) or silence/consonant (unvoiced). If it gets this wrong, it assigns pitch values to silence — contaminating every downstream measurement. pYIN uses probability; CREPE uses its confidence score.	day3::trackers speechprint
Basic	How does CREPE's architecture differ from Praat and pYIN?	CREPE feeds raw 1024-sample waveform windows into a 6-layer CNN. It outputs a 360-bin log-frequency probability distribution. No autocorrelation. No harmonic assumption. It learned pitch from data.	day3::trackers speechprint
Cloze	CREPE uses a {{c1::6}}-layer deep convolutional neural network operating on a {{c2::1024}}-sample raw waveform window. Its confidence score replaces the need for a {{c3::separate voiced/unvoiced detector}}.	Published at ICASSP 2018 by NYU MARL (Music and Audio Research Laboratory).	day3::trackers speechprint
Cloze	CREPE hop length: {{c1::10 ms}} = {{c2::160}} samples at 16 kHz. pYIN hop: {{c3::256}} samples at 44.1 kHz ≈ {{c4::5.8 ms}}.	Finer hop = more frames. CREPE has fewer frames but equal proportional voiced rate.	day3::trackers speechprint
Basic	The tracker ranking from most to least accurate — and one phrase for each.	1. CREPE — neural, no harmonic assumption, best for unusual phonation. 2. pYIN — probabilistic, best for production/real-time. 3. Praat AC + Xu(1999) — good fallback, known octave errors. 4. YIN — no V/UV detection, unreliable. 5. Praat SCC — systematic octave errors, not recommended.	day3::trackers speechprint

Basic	What is MAD and why replace std with it? (optimisation 1)	Median Absolute Deviation. Standard deviation is pulled by outlier movements (very fast accents). MAD is based on the median, so outliers cannot inflate it. Multiplied by 1.4826 to make it consistent with σ under Gaussian assumption. Effect: strong movements 30.9% → 21.0%.	day4::optimisations speechprint
Basic	What is the utterance boundary reset? (optimisation 2)	Syllables across utterance breaks are no longer compared as neighbours. The last syllable of an utterance undergoes final lowering (systematically low F0). Comparing it to the first syllable of the next utterance (reset high) produces a false height contrast. Fix: use tx@TA (DoReCo) or sentence end times as break points.	day4::optimisations speechprint
Basic	What is declination and why does it cause problems? (optimisation 3)	F0 naturally drifts downward across an utterance — the voice relaxes. Without correction, this drift is labelled as falling movements. Fix: fit a linear regression to F0 values within each utterance, subtract the slope. Effect: falls 26.7% → 18.8%.	day4::optimisations speechprint
Basic	What is nucleus edge trimming and why 15%? (optimisation 4)	The first and last 15% of the vowel nucleus window are skipped before sampling F0. At those edges, the vocal tract is still transitioning from/to adjacent consonants — pitch is unstable there. Effect: slight increase in unknowns (0.9% → 1.5%), which is correct — short nuclei now honestly flagged.	day4::optimisations speechprint
Basic	What is octave recovery in Xu(1999)? (optimisation 5)	Before discarding a flagged outlier frame, try multiplying and dividing by 2. If either octave alternative passes the 12-semitone threshold, keep the best one. Recovers data rather than discarding it.	day4::optimisations speechprint
Cloze	The 5 optimisations in order: {{c1::MAD threshold}} · {{c2::utterance boundary reset}} · {{c3::declination removal}} · {{c4::nucleus edge trimming}} · {{c5::octave recovery}}.	Mnemonic: My Buddy Definitely Enjoys Oranges	day4::optimisations speechprint
Cloze	MAD = 1.4826 × median(|xᵢ − median(x)|). The 1.4826 factor makes MAD equivalent to {{c1::σ (standard deviation)}} under a {{c2::Gaussian}} assumption.	This is standard in robust statistics. The factor is the reciprocal of the normal distribution's quantile function at 0.75.	day4::optimisations speechprint
Cloze	Declination fix: fit {{c1::linear regression}} to F0 values within each utterance as a function of {{c2::time}}, then subtract the {{c3::slope}} (keeping the intercept).	Removes the temporal trend while preserving the absolute F0 level for accent detection.	day4::optimisations speechprint

Basic	What are the five German GToBI sentences and their tone patterns?	eine gelbe Banane (L+H*) · einige Melonen (H+L*) · er sang die Lieder (H+!H*) · er will die Rosen haben (L*+H) · ich wohne in Bern (L*+H)	day5::gtobi speechprint
Basic	What is the L*+H pattern and why does it fail?	A rising accent where the starred tone is LOW and the high peak comes on the FOLLOWING syllable. SpeechPrint assigns accents based on local amplitude and F0 height — it finds the acoustically prominent syllable, not the lexically starred one. Fix: add a one-syllable look-ahead window.	day5::gtobi speechprint
Basic	What MFA errors were found and what was the fix?	MFA wrong transcriptions: "gelbe"→"gerbe", "einige Melonen"→"einigen mal lohnt", "er sang die Lieder"→"hesangli lieder". MFA timing also off 66–430 ms from human Wort in all 5 sentences. Fix: abandoned MFA entirely. Hardcoded correct German IPA; distributed proportionally within human Wort boundaries.	day5::gtobi speechprint
Basic	Why does CREPE score 1/5 correct while pYIN scores 3/5 — and which is actually better?	pYIN wins the binary correct/incorrect score. CREPE gets the pitch movement direction right on all 5 sentences — the 4 partials have correct direction and correct word, just no accent marker (*). The accent marker requires 1.5 dB relative loudness, which is rarely triggered in short 1–1.5s sentences. CREPE is more interpretable; pYIN is more accurate on this metric.	day5::gtobi speechprint
Basic	Why does CREPE not trigger the * accent in short sentences?	Short sentences have small relative amplitude differences between syllables. The 1.5 dB threshold for accent detection is calibrated for longer utterances. In 1–1.5s recordings, even the most prominent syllable may not exceed its neighbours by 1.5 dB. Future fix: recalibrate threshold for short utterances.	day5::gtobi speechprint
Cloze	The corrected syllabification for einige Melonen: {{c1::aɪ·nɪ·ɡə·me·loː·nən}} (6 syllables). Before MFA correction: {{c2::ei·ni·gen·mal·lohnt}} (5, wrong).	MFA had merged and mis-transcribed. Correct IPA from known German text gives linguistically accurate syllabification.	day5::gtobi speechprint
Cloze	The GToBI Ton tier is a {{c1::TextTier}} (point tier), not an IntervalTier.	Each tone event is stored as a single time point + label. We convert it to an interval tier by mapping each point to the syllable interval it falls within.	day5::gtobi speechprint

Basic	What is the DoReCo corpus and what is Daakie?	DoReCo is a documentary corpus of endangered languages with detailed human-annotated TextGrids. Daakie is an Oceanic language spoken in Port Vato, Vanuatu. The recording is 162.7 s from a single speaker.	day6::doreco speechprint

Basic	What is the SAMPA phone inventory like for Daakie?	Vowels: a, a:, e:, E, E:, i, i:, O, O:, o, u, u:, {. Consonants include labialized variants (b_w, m_w, p_w) and affricates (ts\). Not standard IPA — DoReCo-specific notation built on SAMPA.	day6::doreco speechprint

Basic	What does ‾ mean? What does _ mean?	‾ (overline, U+203E): syllable mean F0 is above neighbouring syllables. _ (underscore): syllable mean F0 is below neighbouring syllables. They are visual opposites — above and below the text baseline.	day8::symbols speechprint
Basic	What does * mean? What three cues trigger it?	* marks a prominent accent. Triggered when the syllable is ≥1.5 dB louder than neighbours AND (either ≥1 ST higher in F0 OR ≥1.25× longer in duration). Three-cue definition: louder, higher, longer.	day8::symbols speechprint
Basic	What does // mean vs /? What does \\\\ mean vs \?	// = strongly rising pitch within the syllable (movement ≥ 2.5× weak threshold). / = weakly rising. \\\\ = strongly falling. \\ = weakly falling. Direction is measured as ΔF₀ = 12·log₂(offset/onset) in semitones.	day8::symbols speechprint
Basic	Give an example of each combined symbol and what it means.	*‾// = prominent, high, strongly rising (L+H* type) · _\\\\ = low, strongly falling · ‾\\ = high, weakly falling · _// = low, strongly rising (L*+H contour) · ? = unvoiced or insufficient F0 data	day8::symbols speechprint
Cloze	The adaptive threshold: weak_thr = max({{c1::0.5}} ST, {{c2::0.35}} × MAD_normalised). Strong movement = {{c3::2.5}}× weak_thr. Floor prevents {{c4::extremely narrow-range speakers}} from having no movements labelled.	The floor of 0.5 ST ensures even quiet, flat speech gets some direction labels.	day8::symbols speechprint
Basic	Why relative and not absolute measurements?	Absolute values carry no information about prominence in context. A syllable at 130 Hz could be high in a male voice and low in a female voice. Relative to neighbours: Mary is 4.2 ST above local context (strongly prominent); flew is 2.1 ST above (less prominent). Absolute measure cannot distinguish them. Relative amplitude: reduced false-positive accent rate from ~35% to ~24%.	day8::symbols speechprint
Cloze	An octave error adds {{c1::±12 ST}} to ΔF₀. A mild fall of −2 ST becomes {{c2::+10 ST}} → labelled {{c3::strongly rising}}. This is why octave errors directly invert prosody labels.	The formula is ΔF₀ = 12·log₂(offset/onset). Halving the onset adds +12 ST.	day8::symbols speechprint

Basic	What are the 9 pipeline stages in order?	1. Load audio · 2. Transcribe (WhisperX large-v3) · 3. Clean transcript · 4. Force-align (MFA for English) · 5. Extract F0 + intensity (pYIN + Parselmouth 40ms window) · 6. Phonemize (espeak-ng → IPA) · 7. Label prosody (adaptive thresholds + CREPE for DoReCo) · 8. Write TextGrid · 9. Export CSV + JSON	day9::mixed speechprint
Basic	What is final lowering and why does it matter for the boundary reset?	Final lowering is the phonetic phenomenon whereby F0 drops toward the end of an intonational phrase as the speaker's voice relaxes. The last syllable of an utterance is systematically lower than the same syllable mid-utterance. Without the boundary reset, this low syllable is compared to the first syllable of the next utterance (which resets high) — producing a spurious height contrast.	day9::mixed speechprint
Basic	What is the difference between pYIN and YIN in one sentence each?	pYIN: probabilistic — gives a confidence score for each frame, can output "unvoiced." YIN: deterministic — always outputs a frequency, even for silence. YIN's 97.7% voiced rate on Daakie is a red flag.	day9::mixed speechprint
Basic	The GToBI pattern that both pipelines fail on — and why — and the fix.	L*+H — delayed peak. The accent star is on a low syllable; the high peak arrives on the following syllable. SpeechPrint assigns accents based on local amplitude and F0 height — it finds the acoustically high syllable, not the lexically starred one. Fix: one-syllable look-ahead window in the accent assignment logic.	day9::mixed speechprint
Basic	Name the two BEST folders and what each contains.	DORECO_BEST/: one 162.7s Daakie TextGrid + MP4 video + build.py. 8 tiers: sentence, translation, words, gloss, syllables, phones, prosody. GTOBI_BEST/: five German sentence TextGrids + summary PNG + build.py. 8 tiers: sentence, translation, words, gloss, syllables, phones, gtobi, prosody.	day9::mixed speechprint
Cloze	The Xu(1999) spike threshold is {{c1::12 semitones}} = {{c2::one octave}}.	12 ST is the exact size of an octave error. Any deviation larger than this from the utterance median is flagged as a spike.	day9::mixed speechprint
Cloze	For nucleus edge trimming: trim {{c1::15%}} from each end. Remaining window must be at least {{c2::10 ms}} or fall back to {{c3::full nucleus}}.	Consonant-to-vowel and vowel-to-consonant transitions contaminate the first and last ~10–15 ms of the vowel nucleus.	day9::mixed speechprint
