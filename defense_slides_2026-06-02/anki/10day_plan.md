# 10-Day Defense Plan
### SpeechPrint · June 2026 · Lydia Krifka-Dobes
### Goal: know it by heart, not by reading

---

## The principle

You do not memorise a speech. You understand it deeply enough that the
words come naturally when you talk about what you understand.
The Anki cards are not for memorising sentences — they are for making
the facts feel as automatic as your own name.

One rule: **never practise by reading the speaker notes aloud.**
That trains you to need the notes. Instead, talk to an imaginary friend.

---

## Day 1 (today) — Read only, no cards

**Do:** Read the intuition_guide.md top to bottom. Slowly.
**Do:** For each concept, pause and say it back in your own words out loud.
**Do not:** Open the speaker notes. Do not practise the slides yet.

Tonight before bed: close your eyes and say the Big Story sentence.
> "Writing captures words. Speech has prosody — pitch, stress, rhythm.
> SpeechPrint recovers that. Automatically. Any language. Two minutes."

---

## Day 2 — Numbers only (Anki, cloze cards)

**Do:** Import speechprint_anki.txt into Anki. Study only the **Cloze** cards.
**Target:** Get through all 22 cloze cards at least once.
**Trick:** When you get one wrong, say the correct answer three times out loud.

Focus today on just these five numbers until they feel obvious:
- 238 (English octave errors)
- 9,912 / 60.9% (CREPE voiced frames)
- 30.9% → 21.0% (strong movements, MAD fix)
- 26.7% → 18.8% (falls, declination fix)
- 3/5 (GToBI)

---

## Day 3 — Concept cards (Anki, Basic cards)

**Do:** Study the **Basic** Q&A cards in Anki. Don't rush.
**Target:** All 30 Basic cards at least once.

Pick three cards whose answers surprised you or that you fumbled.
Tonight: explain those three concepts to your mirror as if to a curious
non-linguist. Not the Anki answer — your own words.

---

## Day 4 — The technical slides, eyes closed

**Do:** Open slides 10, 18, 18b, 21b. Look at each image for 30 seconds.
Close your laptop. Talk about what you just saw for 2 minutes.

Start with **slide 18b** (tracker table + optimisation chart) —
this is the slide with the most specific facts.

Practice order (hardest first):
1. Slide 18b — tracker numbers + before/after chart
2. Slide 21b — five optimisations (use mnemonic: My Buddy Definitely Enjoys Oranges)
3. Slide 10 — the symbols
4. Slide 18 — key finding + two-pipeline conclusion

---

## Day 5 — The story arc

**Do:** For each of the 25 slides, say ONE sentence that captures it.
Then say what the NEXT slide is about and why you are moving there.

The transition matters more than the content. If you know why you
are moving from slide A to slide B, you will never lose your place.

Write your own one-liners here (handwriting helps memory):

```
Slide 1:  _______________________________________________
Slide 2:  _______________________________________________
Slide 3:  _______________________________________________
...
Slide 18b: _______________________________________________
Slide 21b: _______________________________________________
```

---

## Day 6 — First full run-through

**Do:** Talk through the full presentation out loud. Time it. Aim for 30 min.
**Do:** Use the slides as visual anchors only — do not read anything.
**Do not:** Stop when you fumble. Keep going. Note the fumbles on paper.

After: look at your fumble list. Those are tomorrow's Anki reviews.

---

## Day 7 — The hard questions

**Do:** Ask yourself (or ask a friend to ask you):
- "Why CREPE for Daakie specifically?"
- "Is 3/5 good enough?"
- "What is declination and did you expect to find it?"
- "What is the actual contribution?"
- "What would you do differently?"

Answer each one in 60–90 seconds. Time yourself with a phone.

**Do:** Review only the cards you got wrong in Anki on days 2–3.

---

## Day 8 — Second full run-through + record yourself

**Do:** Record yourself on your phone doing the full talk.
Watch it once. You will cringe. That is normal and useful.

Look for: moments where you speed up (anxiety), filler words,
moments where you lose eye contact with an imaginary audience.

Fix one thing. Only one. Do not spiral.

---

## Day 9 — Light day

**Do:** Anki cards only, 20 minutes. No full run-through.
**Do:** Say the Big Story out loud in the morning and before sleep.
**Do:** Go for a walk and talk through slide 21b (optimisations) in your head.
**Do:** Eat properly. Sleep 8 hours.

---

## Day 10 (day before defense) — Very light

**Do:** One slow run-through of just the technical slides (9, 10, 18, 18b, 21b, 22).
**Do:** Read the "questions you will probably get" section in intuition_guide.md.
**Do not:** Drill cards. Do not read speaker notes. Do not practise for more than 45 min.
**Do:** Do something you enjoy in the evening. Your brain consolidates during rest.

---

## On the day

Before you go in:
- Say the Big Story sentence quietly to yourself.
- Say the mnemonic for the 5 optimisations: "My Buddy Definitely Enjoys Oranges"
- Take three slow breaths.

During:
- If you lose your place: look at the slide. One image = one idea.
  The slide is your anchor, not your script.
- If you go blank: pause, breathe, say "what this means in practice is..."
  That phrase buys you three seconds and sounds intentional.
- If someone asks a hard question: it is OK to say
  "That is a good question — let me think for a moment."

---

## The secret

The examiner is not testing whether you can recite facts.
They are testing whether you understand what you built.
If you know WHY you made every decision, the words will come.

The thesis is yours. You made it. No one in the room knows it better than you.
